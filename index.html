Design Philosophy

My 3 mantras for successful design

[1]
⚡

Instinctive

Design should be instinctive for the user without relying on instructions. If you need to explain it, redesign it.

[2]
↻

Iterative

There is no finish line to design with each iteration, an opportunity to get it better for the user.

[3]

Open

About

I'm a Senior Experience Designer based in beautiful Vancouver, British Columbia.

I started my career lost in lines of JavaScript as a front-end engineer, but living in Tokyo changed everything. Seeing how thoughtful design permeated every aspect of daily life—from convenience store packaging to the intricate subway system—prompted me to trade my keyboard for a sketchbook and leap into product design.

With 10+ years of experience across consulting, e-commerce, education, healthcare, and the gaming industry, I specialize in turning messy, complicated problems into simple, intuitive experiences. I believe great design is instinctive, iterative, and driven by a collaborative approach to uncover clarity hidden within complexity.

Mentorship & Industry Leadership Design is as much about craft as it is about community. I am a regular industry speaker, a Lead UX Instructor, and a judge for hackathons across Vancouver at universities like UBC, SFU, and Emily Carr. Whether I'm guiding emerging designers, helping students refine their portfolios, or evaluating innovative hackathon projects, I see these moments as my way to give back and help shape the future of design.

Outside of Work You'll find me passionately cheering on Liverpool in their quest for EPL glory, experimenting in the kitchen for my wife (results vary), indulging my cinephile side, or playing badminton at the local rec center.
