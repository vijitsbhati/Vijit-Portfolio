Case Study 01

EYQ – Enterprise Generative AI Platform

Designing a centralized AI marketplace enabling practitioners to create, manage, and safely use AI-powered “skills” for consulting workflows.

Experience StrategyPlatform DesignAI GovernanceB2B Enterprise
Role
UX Lead
Contributions
Research, UX Strategy, UI Design
Team
1 PM · 6 Eng · AI Teams
Platform
Web Platform
The Problem

Consultants were experimenting with external generative AI tools like ChatGPT, which lacked governance and security for sensitive client data. There was no way to standardize prompts, share workflows, or track output accuracy securely across the global firm.

The Goal

Instead of a traditional chatbot, build a centralized AI marketplace where teams could create reusable "Skills", manage prompt libraries, and safely scale AI capabilities across the organization while providing Admins full oversight.

01

Understanding the Ecosystem

We began by defining the three core personas the platform needed to support. A critical design insight quickly emerged: The platform needed to function as a governed ecosystem, not just a chat interface. By analyzing internal workflows, we identified distinct pain points and motivations for each user group.

Client Practitioners
Consulting, Tax, and Assurance professionals needing to augment daily tasks, streamline workflows, and access firm-approved resources without risking data security.
Goal: Streamline Workflows
EY Developers
Technical builders focused on creating and integrating AI solutions via low-code tools. They need clear governance processes for publishing skills and integrating with existing business systems.
Goal: Create Reusable Assets
Platform Admins
Managers responsible for tracking AI performance, monitoring user engagement, handling feedback, and ensuring strict data governance and compliance across global teams.
Goal: Monitor & Govern
02

Experience Strategy & Concepting

We defined core user journeys around selecting an existing skill, creating a custom skill, and monitoring usage.

System Architecture
Designed user flows allowing teams to build skills using contextual data, share with colleagues, and test outputs before publishing.
Low-Fidelity Concepting
Explored core interactions: the AI skill marketplace, prompt library management, and context-aware chat interactions.

Wireframes & Structural Ideation

I utilized wireframes to validate layout mechanics and interaction patterns for prompt discovery, chatbot behaviors, and system configuration before moving into high-fidelity.

03

High-Fidelity Design: Discovery & Onboarding

To drive adoption among Client Practitioners, the platform needed to feel approachable and immediately valuable. We designed a clear onboarding flow to introduce the concept of "Skills" alongside standard chat functionality.

The welcoming onboarding modal introducing the concept of a curated Skills library to users.
The main landing view, allowing users to toggle seamlessly between general 'EYQ Chat' and domain-specific 'Skills Chat'.
Detailed skill profile pages help users understand the specific use-case and accuracy rating before invoking the AI.
04

High-Fidelity Design: The Conversational Interface

The core interaction takes place within the chat interface. We designed a system where users are not staring at a blank screen; instead, they are presented with contextual "Best Practices" and prompt suggestions based on the specific skill they have selected.

The 'Skills Chat' welcome screen provides immediate, actionable prompt templates tailored to the selected domain.
Tooltips guide users on when to use standard AI versus expert, specialized skills.
An active chat session showing clear distinctions between the user's input and the AI assistant's response.
The persistent input area allows users to continue refining their prompts seamlessly.
05

High-Fidelity Design: Prompt Management

To reduce repetitive work and share knowledge, we introduced a Prompt Management library. This acts as a repository where practitioners can find, favorite, and "Try out" highly effective prompts created by colleagues across the firm.

The Prompt Management dashboard, allowing users to browse pre-tested queries by service line or sector.
06

High-Fidelity Design: Empowering Creators

A significant challenge for EY Developers was the lack of scalable low-code tools to build these models. I designed a streamlined "Add Skill" flow that broke down complex prompt engineering and model configuration into intuitive, step-by-step form layouts. This allowed developers to assign regions, tags, and specific prompt hosts with ease.

Step 1: Establishing global properties, targeted user groups, and specific service lines for the AI Skill.
Step 2: Connecting the skill to specific host URLs and defining core prompts before publishing.

Once a skill is created, developers need to see how it performs in the wild. I designed a detailed Skill profile and Analytics view. This gives creators immediate feedback on Accuracy, Execution Time, and user sentiment (Likes/Dislikes) so they can continually refine their models.

The Skill Details view gives creators an immediate snapshot of accuracy and execution metrics.
The Analytics tab provides charts for Discovery Time and Response Time to monitor technical latency.
07

High-Fidelity Design: Governance & Admin

For the Platform Admins, visibility was key. Without data, they couldn't govern the platform. I designed a comprehensive Admin Dashboard that aggregates data across the entire EYQ ecosystem. Admins can track overarching adoption rates, identify the top-performing skills, and dig into specific user feedback to identify toxic or hallucinated outputs.

The centralized Admin Dashboard tracking total active users, top 5 skills, and global response time metrics.

To handle frustrations regarding inconsistent report quality, we built a dedicated Platform Feedback interface. This allows Admins to filter through hundreds of direct user reviews across different countries. By centralizing the feedback loop, Admins can quickly deprecate failing skills or reward highly rated ones.

Zero visibility into how models performed or if they hallucinated in client scenarios.
Centralized Governance. Admins can now audit specific interactions and export data for compliance reviews.
Granular review tracking to ensure the AI remains compliant and helpful.
08

Outcomes & Reflections

Rapid Onboarding due to a simplified user experience tailored specifically for enterprise workflows.
Increased Creation of reusable AI skills scaling seamlessly across multiple consulting teams.
Higher Adoption of secure internal AI tools compared to risky external alternatives.

Designing for Ecosystems, Not Features: Enterprise AI tools require platform thinking, where value comes from reusable capabilities rather than isolated features.

Governance Is Critical: Balancing rapid innovation with strict security and compliance shaped many of the core design decisions we made, especially for the Admin and Developer personas.

Adoption Depends on Clear Value: Providing visible benefits — such as reusable skills and automation of tedious consulting workflows — was absolutely essential to driving adoption across the firm.

Case Study 02

SaaS Contract Analyzer

Augmenting the financial auditing process for SaaS Contracts at EY by designing an application that identifies and annotates financially impactful sections.

AI/ML UXB2B SaaSFintechPrototyping
Role
UX Lead
Team
2 PMs, 2 Data Sci, 6 Devs
Goal
Audit Time: 4wks → 1wk
Platform
Web · Enterprise
The Struggle

Auditors review multiple contracts daily, each exceeding 300 pages. Combing through these manually takes countless hours. Since SaaS contracts generally use a reference document to verify the language of financially impacting sections, an opportunity arose to train an ML model on past annotations to reduce manual labor.

The Goal

To design a machine-learning-assisted workflow that reduces the auditing process of a SaaS Contract from 4-5 weeks down to 1-2 weeks, significantly cutting the required person-hours.

Visualizing the manual contract analysis process.
01

Brainstorming & Ideation

I facilitated a virtual design workshop on Mural with Auditors, ML Experts, PMs, and the Tech Lead to define the problem statement and identify high-priority functions.

Virtual design workshop defining problem statements and high-priority functions.
Human-in-the-Loop
Users needed the ability to confidently approve or reject machine-generated recommendations before they were finalized.
Scalable Architecture
The solution needed to be designed so it could eventually scale across different departments for various document types.
Manual Categorization
Even with ML, auditors required the fallback ability to manually add, highlight, and categorize clauses within the documents.
02

Design Iterations

Based on the workshop findings, I conducted a collaborative sketching session to get insights into what the stakeholders aspired for in their ideal auditing workflow. I designed the wireframes based on the most voted sketches to test with users, focusing on the auditing workflow and the experience with the machine-identified clauses in the contracts.

Initial wireframing focusing on the interaction with machine-identified clauses.

After finalizing the feature set with the PMs and auditing team, I moved to high-fidelity using our in-house Motif design system. After 2 rounds of iteration testing with stakeholders and business, the designs were handed off to developers.

Engagement page for contract setup.
Contract analysis view displaying annotated text.
03

Overcoming the ML Hurdle

During stakeholder feedback, the model successfully identified all financial sections (a huge win!), but was categorizing most annotations incorrectly into the "other" field.

"There is still a lot of manual work required to categorize the clauses correctly."
— Auditing Users

As user retention and adoption were of concern due to the manual effort needed to enhance the Machine Learning Model's accuracy, our team selected 6 power users to test the platform for a month before the company-wide launch. To oversee progress, I created admin screens to monitor model performance and track user metrics.

Internal team dashboard for tracking model performance.
Detailed usage metrics for power users during the beta period.
04

Outcomes & Reflections

4w→1wAudit Time Reduced
92%ML Model Accuracy
80%Auditor Adoption
Achieved an 80% ML model accuracy in the first month, which soared to 92% after just four months of company-wide launch.
Adopted by 80% of financial auditors across EY, accounting for roughly 50% of the revenue generated by the auditing department.

Early Dev Involvement: Bringing developers into early brainstorming sessions severely reduces back-and-forth caused by feasibility restrictions, especially with complex ML implementations.

Managing Scope Creep: Scope creep is a recurring threat during stakeholder check-ins; it is vital to confidently steer discussions back within the defined constraints.

Horizontal Scalability: The massive success of this tool led other teams to request similar solutions, resulting in a new product adapted for various departments.

Case Study 03

Listing Doctor

Designing an application for the listing quality team at Spreetail to seamlessly track and fix listing errors on Amazon, Walmart, eBay and other online marketplaces.

UX ResearchPrototypingWorkshop FacilitationUsability TestingE-commerce
Role
UX Lead
Team
1 PM, 1 Tech Lead, 5 Devs
Goal
82% → 95% Active Listings
Platform
Web App · SaaS
The Struggle

Spreetail's listing quality team fell behind in keeping up with listing errors for hundreds of new products across multiple marketplaces. This was costing the company millions in inactive listings. The fix wasn't the problem — it was the tedious task of combing through mismatched error formats dumped into excel sheets from various APIs.

The Goal

To design a solution that streamlines the identification, tracking, and resolution of these errors, ultimately aiming to increase the percentage of active listings from 82% to more than 95% across all marketplaces.

The tedious task of combing through mismatched error formats from various APIs.
01

Discovery Workshops

I facilitated a cross-functional design workshop that utilized Affinity Mapping, Crazy 8 sketching, and dot voting. I brought together Marketplace Managers, Operations, Vendor Managers, and the Listing Quality Team to brainstorm issues and align on solutions.

Affinity mapping and identifying high-priority problems.
Unified Overview Needed
Users needed a clean, aggregated overview of listing errors for each individual marketplace, rather than raw excel exports.
Financial Visibility
There was a strong desire to view the direct financial impact of listings with unresolved errors to help prioritize the workload.
Claiming & Noting
The quality team needed the ability to "claim" specific listing errors for resolution and attach notes for ongoing follow-ups.
02

Design Iterations & Testing

Based on the workshop findings, I partnered with the PM to map a user journey and transform ideation data into low-fidelity wireframes to test with users, focusing on identifying different error categories across marketplaces. Walkthroughs were a success, allowing us to pivot quickly.

Low-fidelity wireframe establishing the core layout.
Wireflow demonstrating the error categorization process.

After testing, I created high-fidelity designs using our in-house Meta design system. After 4 distinct rounds of iterations, the designs were handed off, followed by weekly sync-ups with developers to navigate feasibility constraints.

High-fidelity dashboard showcasing error metrics.
03

Soft Launch Insights & Solutions

We launched a beta version to a select group of 8 users representing different personas. While users praised the simplicity and clear error messaging, a major challenge arose.

"We want to download an excel report of all the errors that we can work through."
— Beta Users
Users struggled to prioritize their workload based solely on the volume of errors presented in the dashboard list.
Business Impact View. Created a separate view focused entirely on error metrics and their impact on business revenue, catering to both the quality and marketplace teams.
Users were restricted to exploring errors via generic categories, disrupting their existing habits.
Mental Model Alignment. Redesigned the search functionality to allow users to search directly via item numbers, aligning with their existing mental model.
Revised layout incorporating search by item number.
Detailed error resolution panel.
Financial impact view for prioritization.
04

Outcomes & Reflections

82%→95%Active Listings Reached
100%Quarterly Goals Met
4Design Iterations

After another series of user testing, Listing Doctor was successfully launched to the entire user base! Within the first 3 months, the listing quality team saw an increase in their productivity, allowing them to meet their quarterly metric goals of resolved listing errors.

API Independence: Finding solutions to track listing status that reduce our strict dependence on delayed API responses from marketplaces is a must moving forward.

Empowering the Team: Building a system where the quality team can actively claim errors to fix—instead of blindly relying on system priority—proved to be key for engagement.

Change Management: Focusing heavily on onboarding users by clearly highlighting the key benefits was critical in getting them to adapt to an entirely new process.

Case Study 04

Wingman

Integrating OpenAI's ChatGPT technology into a travel companion iOS application to provide instant, personalized information in a conversational way.

Conversational UIiOS DesignGen AIUsability Testing
Role
UX Designer
Team
1 Partner, 1 PM
Goal
Secure M&A Investment
Platform
iOS · Mobile
The Challenge

To demonstrate the "art of the possible" to a Mergers and Acquisitions firm by showcasing how innovative conversational chat UI can seamlessly integrate disparate travel services into a single, cohesive user experience.

The Goal

Design a high-fidelity conversational prototype to secure the M&A firm's support and investment in selected companies, whose services were simulated as seamless integrations within our chat interface.

01

Designing for Conversation

Step 1: User-Based Context Research
Extensive online research was conducted to comprehend the wide variety of user needs within a comprehensive airport environment. This ranged from requirements like information on connecting flights and lounge facilities, to details about boarding gates and food & beverage options.
Establishing context and user needs at the airport.
Step 2: Technology Integration
The imminent task was then to integrate OpenAI’s Chatbot GPT technology into the existing app design. This integration was aimed at offering personalized assistance to travelers, enhancing their overall airport experience.
Conversational AI taking over complex filtering.
Step 3: User-Centric Design
Following a user-centered design approach, I focused on usability and user feedback from the original application design to guarantee seamless interaction between the mobile application and its users.
Step 4: Accessibility Considerations
In designing the application, universal design principles were strictly adhered to. This ensured maximum usability and accessibility for a diverse range of users. Elements such as color contrast, simple text, recognizable icons, voice-enabled AI, and an intuitive interface were incorporated.
Voice-enabled AI and accessible conversational UI.
02

Testing & Validation

I conducted usability tests using the high-fidelity prototype with four stakeholder participants. Throughout the test, observations and feedback were carefully noted, encompassing aspects related to the ChatGPT feature's utility and usability. Each session concluded with a debriefing where additional feedback was gathered. The feedback from these usability tests was used while presenting this prototype to the M&A firm.

03

Outcomes & Success

100%Stakeholder Buy-In
4/4Usability Success
ActiveM&A Negotiations
A Resounding Success: The presentation successfully achieved its goal of securing support from the M&A firm for investing in the selected target companies.
Moving Forward: Thanks to the tangible demonstration of the integrated UI, the team immediately moved into the active negotiation phase with the respective companies.
