Case Study 01

EYQ – Enterprise Generative AI Platform

Designing a centralized AI marketplace enabling practitioners to create, manage, and safely use AI-powered “skills” for consulting workflows.

Experience StrategyPlatform DesignAI GovernanceB2B Enterprise
Role
UX Lead
Contributions
Research, UX Strategy, Wireframing
Team
1 PM · 6 Eng · AI Teams
Platform
Web Platform
The Problem

Consultants were experimenting with external generative AI tools like ChatGPT, which lacked governance and security for sensitive client data. There was no way to standardize prompts, share workflows, or track output accuracy.

The Goal

Instead of a traditional chatbot, build a centralized AI marketplace where teams could create reusable skills, manage prompt libraries, and safely scale AI capabilities across the organization while tracking adoption.

01

User Research & Personas

We began by defining the three core personas the platform needed to support. A critical design insight quickly emerged: The platform needed to function as a skills ecosystem, not just a chatbot.

Client Practitioners
Consultants needing quick access to trusted, reusable AI capabilities to automate tasks and improve day-to-day efficiency.
EY Developers
Technical users building AI solutions, integrating models, and publishing reusable skills for business workflows.
Platform Admins
Administrators requiring analytics dashboards to monitor usage, track performance, and ensure governance standards.
02

Experience Strategy & Concepting

We defined core user journeys around selecting an existing skill, creating a custom skill, and starting a direct AI conversation.

System Architecture
Designed user flows allowing teams to build skills using contextual data, share with colleagues, and test outputs before publishing.
Low-Fidelity Concepting
Explored core interactions: the AI skill marketplace, prompt library management, and context-aware chat interactions.
Validation Testing
Ran usability tests to ensure users understood how to activate skills, edit parameters, and switch between general AI chat and specific workflows.
03

High-Fidelity Design & Governance

Isolated prompt engineering restricted to individual users with no oversight on quality or security.
Centralized Skill Marketplace. A unified hub designed using EY's system to create, manage, and share prompt libraries securely.
Zero visibility into how AI models were performing or being utilized across consulting teams.
Admin Analytics Dashboards. Equipped admins to monitor adoption rates, engagement, model performance, and confidence scores.
04

Outcomes & Impact

Rapid Onboarding due to a simplified user experience tailored specifically for enterprise workflows.
Increased Creation of reusable AI skills scaling seamlessly across multiple consulting teams.
Higher Adoption of secure internal AI tools compared to risky external alternatives.
05

Final Reflections

Designing for Ecosystems, Not Features: Enterprise AI tools require platform thinking, where value comes from reusable capabilities rather than isolated features.

Governance Is Critical: Balancing rapid innovation with strict security and compliance shaped many of the core design decisions we made.

Adoption Depends on Clear Value: Providing visible benefits — such as reusable skills and automation of tedious consulting workflows — was absolutely essential to driving adoption across the firm.

Case Study 02

SaaS Contract Analyzer

Augmenting the financial auditing process for SaaS Contracts at EY by designing an application that identifies and annotates financially impactful sections.

AI/ML UXB2B SaaSFintechPrototyping
Role
UX Lead
Team
2 PMs, 2 Data Sci, 6 Devs
Goal
Audit Time: 4wks → 1wk
Platform
Web · Enterprise
The Struggle

Auditors review multiple contracts daily, each exceeding 300 pages. Combing through these manually takes countless hours. Since SaaS contracts generally use a reference document to verify the language of financially impacting sections, an opportunity arose to train an ML model on past annotations to reduce manual labor.

The Goal

To design a machine-learning-assisted workflow that reduces the auditing process of a SaaS Contract from 4-5 weeks down to 1-2 weeks, significantly cutting the required person-hours.

01

Brainstorming & Ideation

I facilitated a virtual design workshop on Mural with Auditors, ML Experts, PMs, and the Tech Lead to define the problem statement and identify high-priority functions.

Human-in-the-Loop
Users needed the ability to confidently approve or reject machine-generated recommendations before they were finalized.
Scalable Architecture
The solution needed to be designed so it could eventually scale across different departments for various document types.
Manual Categorization
Even with ML, auditors required the fallback ability to manually add, highlight, and categorize clauses within the documents.
02

Design Iterations

Collaborative Sketching & Wireframing
Conducted sketching sessions to capture the stakeholders' ideal auditing workflow. Built low-fi wireframes focusing on how auditors would interact with machine-identified clauses.
High-Fidelity & Motif System
Moved to high-fidelity using our in-house Motif design system. Conducted 2 rounds of testing with stakeholders before entering a weekly sync rhythm with developers.
Admin Dashboard Creation
Designed specialized admin screens for the internal team to monitor model performance and track user usage metrics prior to the broader launch.
03

Overcoming the ML Hurdle

During stakeholder feedback, the model successfully identified all financial sections (a huge win!), but was categorizing most annotations incorrectly into the "other" field.

"There is still a lot of manual work required to categorize the clauses correctly."
— Auditing Users
Users faced manual fatigue, threatening retention and adoption if they had to correct the ML model too frequently.
Power-User Beta Program. Selected 6 power users to test and train the platform for a full month. This targeted effort skyrocketed the model's accuracy before the company-wide launch.
04

Outcomes & Reflections

4w→1wAudit Time Reduced
92%ML Model Accuracy
80%Auditor Adoption
Achieved an 80% ML model accuracy in the first month, which soared to 92% after just four months.
Adopted by 80% of financial auditors across EY, accounting for roughly 50% of the revenue generated by the auditing department.

Early Dev Involvement: Bringing developers into early brainstorming sessions severely reduces back-and-forth caused by feasibility restrictions, especially with complex ML implementations.

Managing Scope Creep: Scope creep is a recurring threat during stakeholder check-ins; it is vital to confidently steer discussions back within the defined constraints.

Horizontal Scalability: The massive success of this tool led other teams to request similar solutions, resulting in a new product adapted for various departments.

Case Study 03

Listing Doctor

Designing an application for the listing quality team at Spreetail to seamlessly track and fix listing errors on Amazon, Walmart, eBay and other marketplaces.

UX ResearchPrototypingWorkshop FacilitationUsability TestingE-commerce
Role
UX Lead
Team
1 PM, 1 Tech Lead, 5 Devs
Goal
82% → 95% Active Listings
Platform
Web App · SaaS
The Struggle

Spreetail's listing quality team fell behind in keeping up with listing errors for hundreds of new products across multiple marketplaces. This was costing the company millions in inactive listings. The fix wasn't the problem — it was the tedious task of combing through mismatched error formats dumped into excel sheets from various APIs.

The Goal

To design a solution that streamlines the identification, tracking, and resolution of these errors, ultimately aiming to increase the percentage of active listings from 82% to more than 95% across all marketplaces.

01

Discovery Workshops

I facilitated a cross-functional design workshop that utilized Affinity Mapping, Crazy 8 sketching, and dot voting. I brought together Marketplace Managers, Operations, Vendor Managers, and the Listing Quality Team to brainstorm issues and align on solutions.

Unified Overview Needed
Users needed a clean, aggregated overview of listing errors for each individual marketplace, rather than raw excel exports.
Financial Visibility
There was a strong desire to view the direct financial impact of listings with unresolved errors to help prioritize the workload.
Claiming & Noting
The quality team needed the ability to "claim" specific listing errors for resolution and attach notes for ongoing follow-ups.
02

Design Iterations & Testing

Journey Mapping & Wireframing
Partnered with the PM to map the user journey and transform ideation data into low-fidelity wireframes. Focused heavily on organizing different error categories across various marketplaces.
Wireframe Testing
Walkthroughs with the listing quality team validated our approach. Users successfully identified features that were missing, allowing us to pivot quickly before moving to high fidelity.
High-Fidelity & Handoff
Utilized our in-house Meta design system. After 4 distinct rounds of iterations, the designs were handed off, followed by weekly sync-ups with developers to navigate feasibility constraints.
03

Soft Launch Insights

We launched a beta version to a select group of 8 users representing different personas. While users praised the simplicity and clear error messaging, a major challenge arose.

"We want to download an excel report of all the errors that we can work through."
— Beta Users
Users struggled to prioritize their workload based solely on the volume of errors presented in the dashboard list.
Business Impact View. Created a separate view focused entirely on error metrics and their impact on business revenue, catering to both the quality and marketplace teams.
Users were restricted to exploring errors via generic categories, disrupting their existing habits.
Mental Model Alignment. Redesigned the search functionality to allow users to search directly via item numbers, aligning with their existing mental model.
04

Outcomes & Reflections

82%→95%Active Listings Reached
100%Quarterly Goals Met
4Design Iterations

API Independence: Finding solutions to track listing status that reduce our strict dependence on delayed API responses from marketplaces is a must moving forward.

Empowering the Team: Building a system where the quality team can actively claim errors to fix—instead of blindly relying on system priority—proved to be key for engagement.

Change Management: Focusing heavily on onboarding users by clearly highlighting the key benefits was critical in getting them to adapt to an entirely new process.

Case Study 04

Wingman

Integrating OpenAI's ChatGPT technology into a travel companion iOS application to provide instant, personalized information in a conversational way.

Conversational UIiOS DesignGen AIUsability Testing
Role
UX Designer
Team
1 Partner, 1 PM
Goal
Secure M&A Investment
Platform
iOS · Mobile
The Challenge

To demonstrate the "art of the possible" to a Mergers and Acquisitions firm by showcasing how innovative conversational chat UI can seamlessly integrate disparate travel services into a single, cohesive user experience.

The Goal

Design a high-fidelity conversational prototype to secure the M&A firm's support and investment in selected companies, whose services were simulated as seamless integrations within our chat interface.

01

Designing for Conversation

Step 1: User-Based Context Research
Conducted extensive research to comprehend user needs within a chaotic airport environment—ranging from connecting flight info and lounge facilities to boarding gates and F&B options.
Step 2: Technology Integration
Integrated OpenAI’s ChatGPT logic into the existing app design. The integration aimed to offer highly personalized assistance to travelers, moving beyond standard FAQ bots.
Step 3: User-Centric Design
Focused heavily on usability and feedback from the original application to guarantee seamless interaction between the mobile UI and its users.
Step 4: Accessibility Considerations
Strictly adhered to universal design principles. Incorporated high color contrast, simple text, recognizable icons, and voice-enabled AI to ensure maximum accessibility.
02

Testing & Validation

I conducted rigorous usability tests using the high-fidelity prototype with four key stakeholder participants. Observations encompassing the ChatGPT feature's utility and usability were carefully documented and utilized to polish the prototype before the massive M&A presentation.

03

Outcomes & Success

100%Stakeholder Buy-In
4/4Usability Success
ActiveM&A Negotiations
A Resounding Success: The presentation successfully achieved its goal of securing support from the M&A firm for investing in the selected target companies.
Moving Forward: Thanks to the tangible demonstration of the integrated UI, the team immediately moved into the active negotiation phase with the respective companies.
